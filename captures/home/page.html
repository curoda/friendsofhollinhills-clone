​FRIENDS OF HOLLIN HILLS​

Friends of Hollin Hills (FOHH) actively preserves the historical integrity of our unique Alexandria, Virginia neighborhood through community,  preservation, education and nature stewardship. Our programs and events are designed to inspire involvement and foster a deeper understanding of our Mid-century Modern (MCM) roots.

"​Hollin Hills Parks are our community heartbeat."

Friends of Hollin Hills Enjoy & Support:

🌿 Access to Free Educational Programs
Learn from experts about Mid-century Modern architecture,
the history of Hollin Hills, and the ecological importance of native landscapes. Dive into the stories, designs, and philosophies that
shaped our one-of-a-kind neighborhood.

🌿 Opportunities for Deep Learning
From exclusive architecture talks to curated deep dive experiences
like the Goodman Symposium, take advantage of special opportunities
to learn about our community’s design legacy.

🌿 Strong Community Bonds
Get to know your neighbors and other MCM enthusiasts who care ​about history, design, and the environment as much as you do.

🌿 Stewardship of Over 33 Acres of Parkland
Support the care and preservation of beloved community green spaces like Goodman Park, Voigt Park, Brickelmaier Park, and more. Our parklands are a defining feature of historic Hollin Hills—33 acres of trails and greenery that literally connect our neighborhood. Maintained through your contributions and volunteer efforts, these parks need ongoing care. Please consider donating today to help preserve and repair these vital shared spaces. ​

​🌿 Contributing to a Nationally Recognized Historic District
Your support helps maintain the spirit and legacy of one of the most celebrated modernist communities in the U.S.

Your tax-deductible gift supports community-building, educational, and efforts to foster a healthy local ecosystem.

Hollin Hills Historic Plaques

Thoughtfully designed in stainless steel, these plaques were created specifically for Hollin Hills through a multi-year collaboration between architects, designers, and residents. Each plaque highlights your home’s place within this nationally recognized community while also supporting FOHH through a $100 tax-deductible donation benefiting parks and community programs.

Celebrate Hollin Hills Parks with a donation to Friends of Hollin Hills

You can directly shape the community you love. When you support Friends of Hollin Hills, a 501(c)(3) charitable nonprofit organization, your gift becomes a tax write-off and a powerful investment in the beauty and spirit of Hollin Hills. Your generosity helps fund education, preservation, community events, and the shared green spaces that make this neighborhood such a rare and inspiring place to call home.

​Colorful road and trail signs throughout Hollin Hills this winter invite you to take a moment to celebrate our parks, an important part of our Hollin Hills community.

Maintaining these parks is achieved through the dedicated effort of volunteers who work with the Civic Association of Hollin Hills throughout the year to maintain and nurture them. Friends of Hollin Hills supports these efforts through fundraising and grants to provide improvements like new bridges and facilities, invasive removal and educational programs.  

​Your contribution protects what makes Hollin Hills special while giving you the pride of knowing you made a real difference. We are deeply grateful for your support. You help keep our mid century legacy vibrant, our trails welcoming, and our community connected. Thank you for giving back to the place that gives so much to all of us.

Friends of Hollin Hills Grant Application  

Friends of Hollin Hills awards grants on a rolling basis to support projects that preserve, educate, and strengthen the Hollin Hills Historic District. Funding is available for community-benefit projects such as educational programs, materials, native plants, and services that align with FOHH’s charitable goals of historic preservation, community building, and nature stewardship. Proposals must provide public benefit (not private interest).

HOLLIN HILLS – A GROUNDBREAKING MODERN DEVELOPMENT

The Hollin Hills Historic District was designed by renowned MCM architect Charles Goodman and developed by Robert Davenport between 1949-1971. Hollin Hills intentionally marries irregularly-shaped home lots with existing topography. The MCM architectural style is protected by architectural covenants. The community includes over 480 single-family homes and 30+ acres of dedicated park lands with six unique parks and a wildlife sanctuary. For more information on the Hollin Hills Historic District, check out the Civic Association of Hollin Hills (CAHH).

As a 501(c)(3) charitable nonprofit, FOHH relies on donations, sales, and volunteers to support the Hollin Hills Historic District. All funds stay within the community and each dollar plays a critical role. Since the FOHH is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, your donations are tax deductible! Thank you.
